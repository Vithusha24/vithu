Animals in Sri Lanka

jungle fowl

Cloaked in the emerald embrace of Southeast Asian rainforests, jungle fowl strut a vibrant dance of life. Dazzling roosters, adorned with fiery combs and wattles.

Deer

Deer are elegant and graceful mammals belonging to the family Cervidae, which includes a variety of species such as white-tailed deer, mule deer, elk, and moose.

King-fisher

Kingfishers are a fascinating family of birds known for their vibrant colors, distinctive appearance, and remarkable hunting skills.

Blue bird

Bluebirds, with their enchanting azure plumage and gentle demeanor, are a captivating group of birds that belong to the thrush family, Turdidae.

Elephant

Elephants, revered for their immense size, intelligence, and social complexity, are iconic mammals belonging to the family Elephantidae.

King Vulture

the King Vulture possesses a predominantly white plumage with contrasting black flight feathers and tail, while its head and neck are adorned with vivid hues of orange, red, and yellow.

Explore Further

To delve deeper into the world of Sri Lanka's wildlife, don't miss visiting these renowned national parks:

Yala National Park

This sprawling park in the south is famous for its diverse wildlife, including leopards, elephants, and crocodiles. Guided safaris offer excellent opportunities to observe these creatures in their natural habitat.

Learn more about Wilpattu National Park

Wilpattu National Park

Located in the northwest, Wilpattu boasts one of the highest leopard densities in the world. Explore its vast dry season tanks and dense forests on a jeep safari to catch a glimpse of these elusive predators.
