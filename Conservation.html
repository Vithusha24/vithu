Introduction of wild life department


The Wildlife Department of Sri Lanka (DWC) is in charge of managing and conserving wild animals and their habitats, as well as being the guardian of the amazing natural heritage of the nation. The DWC, which was founded in 1949, is essential to preserving Sri Lanka's amazing biodiversity.

Department of Wildlife Conservation Headquarters

he headquarters is instrumental in fostering collaboration with local communities, non-governmental organizations, and international bodies to address broader conservation challenges. Educational outreach programs and public awareness campaigns may also be initiated to promote a greater understanding of the importance of wildlife conservation. Additionally, the Department of Wildlife Conservation Headquarters plays a vital role in responding to emerging issues such as habitat degradation, poaching, and climate change that pose threats to biodiversity.
