Introduction of Yala National Park


Yala National Park, situated in the southeastern corner of Sri Lanka, stands as a testament to the island nation's rich biodiversity and natural beauty. Established in 1900 as a Game Sanctuary, Yala has evolved into one of the most renowned and visited national parks in the country. Spanning over 979 square kilometers, the park encompasses a diverse range of ecosystems, including dense forests, grasslands, and wetlands, providing a haven for a remarkable array of flora and fauna.
